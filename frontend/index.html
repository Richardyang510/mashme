Bombardier Aerospace - Logistics Streamlining

At Bombardier, I was brought in to re-imagine an inefficient section of a jet aircraft assembly line. Working with both the logistics teams and the guys on the floor, I designed a streamlined storage, assembly, and testing process for the fairing panels of Bombardier private jets. To modernize the logistics department, I programmed a tool to track part shortages and alert users. This is where I first experienced the intersection of engineering and coding, and it led me to where I am today.

Toyota Motor Manufacturing - Pedestrian Radar Investigation

Did you know that car companies have their own detectives? When drivers report reocurring problems with their cars, it's up to a small squad of Quality Control Engineers to trace the problem back to a part, and find out when something went wrong, between the time the car is designed and the time someone drives it home. Just like in a mystery novel, QCEs find clues and track down leads until they find the culprit. As a QCE with Toyota, I investigated and resolved a problem with the pedestrian sensor of the Lexus RX350. This project was a great mix of problem-solving, project management, and data analysis.
